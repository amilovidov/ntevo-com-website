Argo Logo - Bauhaus Minimalist Interpretations

Version 1: Pure Geometric
Pure Geometric Sail: Simplified sail as a single curved quadratic bezier, straight mast, curved hull, and geometric wave pattern. Minimal elements, maximum impact.
Version 2: Abstract Triangular
Abstract Triangular: Sharp geometric triangle sail with accent, circular wave dots instead of lines. Bauhaus emphasis on primary shapes (triangle, circle, line).
Version 3: Deconstructed
Deconstructed Elements: Bauhaus deconstruction with rectangular sail cutout, skewed wave stripes, and diamond sun. Emphasizes construction and form.
Version 4: Circular Motion
Circular Motion: Sail represented by overlapping circles creating negative space. Curved wave arcs. Emphasizes circular Bauhaus forms and movement.
Version 5: Constructivist
Constructivist Angles: Russian Constructivism influence with angular parallelogram sail, zigzag waves, and geometric sun rays. Dynamic diagonal emphasis.
Version 6: Ultra Minimal
Ultra Minimal: Absolute minimum elements - curved sail, single hull line, minimal wave strokes. Pure essence of the ship concept.
Version 7: Layered Composition
Layered Composition: Bauhaus layering with overlapping sail shapes, double hull layers, and nested sun circles. Depth through transparency and overlap.
Version 8: Grid System
Grid System: Strict grid-based design with rectangular sail, square wave blocks, and square sun. Bauhaus grid discipline and modular construction.
