⭐ Why This Works Best

Balanced visual hierarchy: Lighter text (30px, weight 400) allows geometric shapes to be the focal point while maintaining excellent legibility.

Bauhaus authenticity: Matches Bauhaus principle of "form follows function" - shapes are primary, text is supporting element.

Versatile: Works at standard website header sizes (300px+) while maintaining coherence. Scales cleanly down to mobile.

Professional & modern: Lighter weight feels contemporary without being too bold or heavy.

✓ Strengths

  • Best balance between logo and text
  • Text doesn't compete with geometric shapes
  • Increased letter-spacing improves readability
  • Lighter weight = more elegant, less corporate
  • Compact 600px width fits most contexts
  • True to Bauhaus design principles

✗ Challenges

  • Lighter text needs clean background (no busy patterns)
  • May need slight adjustment on colored backgrounds
  • At very small sizes (< 250px), text gets tight
Best for: Primary website header, email signatures, business cards, most professional contexts - versatile all-around choice

✓ Strengths

  • Most compact vertical height (120px)
  • Modern single-line treatment
  • All-caps with letter-spacing = tech/startup vibe
  • Works well in tight horizontal spaces
  • Clean horizontal flow, easy to scan
  • Good for mobile headers (single line)

✗ Challenges

  • Text is quite small (24px) - legibility concern
  • All caps can feel less elegant than mixed case
  • Long single line creates stretched feeling
  • Logo is smallest (120px) - less geometric presence
  • At small sizes, text becomes very hard to read
Best for: Mobile-first designs, tight spaces, modern tech aesthetic, footer logos, secondary placements
